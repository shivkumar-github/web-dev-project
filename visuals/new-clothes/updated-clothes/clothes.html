Why Donate Clothes?

Clothes carry stories, memories, and dreams. When you donate your gently worn garments, you're not just giving fabric; you're offering hope, dignity, and a chance for someone else to start a new chapter in their life. Imagine the smiles your old clothes can bring, the warmth they'll provide, and the confidence they'll instill. Your donation isn't just about decluttering your closet; it's about weaving a tapestry of compassion, one thread of generosity at a time. Together, let's clothe the world with kindness.

Shocking Facts

1. The average American throws away 81 pounds of clothing each year. That's 26 billion pounds of textiles that end up in landfills annually.

2. The fashion industry is the second-largest polluter in the world, right behind oil.

3. It takes 2,700 liters of water to make one cotton shirt. That's enough water for one person to drink for 2.5 years.

4. The fashion industry is responsible for 10% of the world's carbon emissions.

5. The average person buys 68 garments per year but only wears 44 of them.

6. The average garment is worn just 7 times before it's discarded.

7. 85% of all textiles end up in landfills each year.

8. The fashion industry produces 20% of global wastewater.

9. The fashion industry is responsible for 92 million tons of solid waste dumped in landfills each year.

10. The fashion industry is responsible for 20% of all industrial water pollution worldwide.
